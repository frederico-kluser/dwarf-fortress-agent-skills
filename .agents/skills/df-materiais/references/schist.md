## In real life

Schist is a large class of metamorphic rock defined by its texture or 'schistosity'; the rock is foliated in such a way that it can split into small flakes. It is often host to metamorphic gemstones such as garnet, emerald, and ruby.

'Schistosity' is a dwarven term for the mineral-like attractiveness of another dwarf. Example, "Urist rolled a high schistosity score on his stone die."

Schist is not to be confused with filth, nor can you make bricks out of it; as the product of a stonecutter is properly called a block.
